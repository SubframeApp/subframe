When you modify your design after building a prototype, you'll need to regenerate the prototype code to reflect those changes.

## When to update

Update your prototype after:
- Adding or removing elements
- Changing element IDs or structure
- Modifying component properties
- Adjusting layouts or styles that affect interactions

Minor style changes (colors, spacing) usually don't require updates unless they're referenced in your prototype logic.

## Regenerating the prototype

Click **Regenerate** in Prototype Mode. AI analyzes your updated design and annotations, then generates new code incorporating the changes.

## What gets preserved

**Annotations** - All element annotations stay in place

**Chat history** - Your conversation with AI is retained

**Interaction logic** - AI attempts to maintain the same behavior with the updated structure

## What might break

**Renamed elements** - If you rename elements that annotations reference, AI might not reconnect them automatically

**Removed elements** - Annotations on deleted elements are removed

**Restructured layouts** - Significant layout changes may require updating your annotations

## Testing after updates

Always test your prototype after regenerating:
1. Click **Preview** to open the prototype
2. Test all interactions and navigation
3. Check for broken functionality
4. Use chat to fix any issues
