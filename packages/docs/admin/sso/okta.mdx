Single sign-on (SSO) with Okta lets your team use their existing Okta credentials to access Subframe. When enabled, team members sign in through your organization's Okta portal instead of creating separate Subframe passwords.

<Note>
SSO is available on the Custom plan. Contact [sales@subframe.com](mailto:sales@subframe.com) to enable SSO for your team.
</Note>

## How SSO works

Once SSO is configured:

1. Team members go to [app.subframe.com](https://app.subframe.com)
2. Click **Continue with SSO**
3. Enter their work email
4. Get redirected to your Okta sign-in page
5. Sign in with Okta credentials
6. Automatically redirected back to Subframe

First-time users are automatically added to your team. You can optionally enforce SSO-only access, which blocks password-based logins for your domain.

## Prerequisites

**Custom plan** - SSO requires a Custom plan subscription. Reach out to sales to upgrade.

**Okta administrator access** - You'll need admin permissions in Okta to create and configure the Subframe application.

**Email domain** - Your team must use a consistent email domain (e.g., `@yourcompany.com`). SSO matches users by their email domain.

## Configuration steps

SSO configuration requires coordination between Subframe and your Okta administrator. Contact [support@subframe.com](mailto:support@subframe.com) to begin setup.

### 1. Request SSO setup

Email [support@subframe.com](mailto:support@subframe.com) with:
- Your team name in Subframe
- The email domain you want to use for SSO (e.g., `@yourcompany.com`)
- Whether you want to enforce SSO-only login (block passwords)
- Your preferred Okta configuration method (SAML metadata URL or manual)

### 2. Configure Okta application

Your Okta admin will create a new SAML 2.0 application:

**In Okta:**
1. Navigate to **Applications > Applications**
2. Click **Create App Integration**
3. Select **SAML 2.0** and click **Next**
4. Name the app "Subframe"
5. Subframe support will provide:
   - **Single sign on URL** (ACS URL)
   - **Audience URI** (Entity ID)
6. Click **Next** and complete the wizard

**Get metadata:**
- Copy the SAML metadata URL from the Okta app
- Or download the metadata XML file
- Send this to Subframe support

### 3. Subframe configures SSO provider

Subframe support will:
- Create an SSO provider in your Subframe account
- Link your team to the Okta SSO provider
- Configure domain matching for your email domain
- Optionally enable SSO-only enforcement

Setup typically takes 1-2 business days.

### 4. Test SSO login

Once configured, test the integration:

1. Open an incognito/private browser window
2. Go to [app.subframe.com](https://app.subframe.com)
3. Click **Continue with SSO**
4. Enter your work email
5. Complete Okta authentication
6. Verify you land on the Subframe dashboard

<Warning>
Test SSO before enabling SSO-only enforcement. If SSO doesn't work and enforcement is enabled, your team won't be able to sign in.
</Warning>

## User provisioning

### Automatic team joining

When a user signs in with SSO for the first time:
- They're automatically added to your Subframe team
- First SSO user becomes an Admin
- Subsequent users become Editors by default

### Pre-inviting users

You can invite users before they sign in with SSO:

1. Navigate to **Team Settings**
2. Click **Invite team member**
3. Enter their email address (must match SSO domain)
4. Select their role (Admin, Editor, or Viewer)
5. Click **Send invitation**

When they sign in with SSO, they'll get the pre-assigned role instead of the default Editor role.

## SSO-only enforcement

SSO-only enforcement blocks password-based logins for your domain. When enabled:
- Users with your email domain must use SSO
- Password logins are rejected with: "This organization requires single sign-on."
- Google OAuth is also blocked for your domain
- Users without SSO credentials cannot access the team

**To enable SSO-only enforcement**, email [support@subframe.com](mailto:support@subframe.com) after verifying SSO works correctly.

## Managing SSO users

### Role assignment

SSO users get roles based on:
1. **Pre-invited** - If invited before first login, they get the invited role
2. **First user** - First person to sign in becomes Admin
3. **Default** - All other users become Editors

Change roles anytime in **Team Settings** by clicking the role dropdown next to their name.

### Removing users

To remove an SSO user:
1. Navigate to **Team Settings**
2. Find the user in the team member list
3. Click their role and select **Inactive**

Inactive users cannot sign in, even with valid Okta credentials. Their Subframe access is revoked but their Okta account remains active.

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
<Accordion title="This organization requires single sign-on">

SSO-only enforcement is enabled for your domain. Use the **Continue with SSO** button and enter your work email instead of password or Google OAuth.

</Accordion>
<Accordion title="No SSO provider found">

Subframe can't find an SSO configuration for your email domain. Verify the email is correct and matches the configured SSO domain. Contact [support@subframe.com](mailto:support@subframe.com) to verify configuration.

</Accordion>
<Accordion title="Redirect to Okta fails">

Check browser console for errors. Verify your email domain is correct. Try a different browser. Contact [support@subframe.com](mailto:support@subframe.com) if the issue persists.

</Accordion>
<Accordion title="User lands on wrong team">

Each SSO provider links to one team. Users automatically join the linked team. Contact [support@subframe.com](mailto:support@subframe.com) if the team linkage is incorrect.

</Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Limitations

**One team per SSO provider** - Each Okta SSO configuration links to one Subframe team. If you need multiple teams with the same domain, contact support.

**No SCIM provisioning** - Subframe doesn't support automatic user provisioning via SCIM. Users are created on first sign-in (JIT provisioning).

**No role mapping** - Okta groups and roles don't automatically map to Subframe roles. You must manually assign roles in Subframe.

**Domain-based only** - SSO matches users by email domain, not Okta groups or other attributes.
